Line of Sight

Player aid · Apex Legends: The Board Game — condensed from the Core Rules, pp. 23–26 (Line of Sight) & p. 29 (Cover)

How to check Line of Sight

  1. Draw an imaginary bird's-eye line from the center of your space (the source) to the center of the target space (or target intersection).
  2. Find every BLOCKAGE the line crosses (on Crossroads: Building Walls & standalone Walls).
  3. Apply the rules at right. If nothing blocks, you have LoS. A BLOCKAGE blocks both ways — there is never one-way sight.
Cover ≠ blockage. Objects with COVER (Half-Walls, Supply Bins, Respawn Beacons) never block LoS — but a target adjacent to one may be in cover (see panel 5).

Key

  • Source (shooter)
  • Target — has LoS
  • Target — no LoS
  • Wall = BLOCKAGE
  • Half-Wall = COVER
  • Clear LoS
  • Blocked LoS
  • Area with no LoS

1 The basic check p. 23

LoS runs center-to-center. If an effective BLOCKAGE sits on the line, sight is blocked; if the line passes clear (even just below/around a wall), you can see.

Straight into the wall → blocked. Line passing clear below it → LoS.

2 Three quick rules p. 23

  • 45° rule: a BLOCKAGE touched only at its corner has no effect.
  • Connected components: if the line crosses a shared intersection, it only matters if both components are BLOCKAGE.
  • Higher than both: a BLOCKAGE higher than both source and target always blocks.
The line only touches the wall's corner → no effect (clear).

3 The “X” rule pp. 24–25

Used when the BLOCKAGE is not higher than both models. The higher model is the Upper space, the other the Lower space.

  1. From the Upper space, find the farthest same-level BLOCKAGE edge on the line. (If it's an edge of your own space, distance = 0 → it doesn't count.)
  2. Extend that edge into a line. X = the number of spaces from the Upper space to that line — count the gap, not the space your Legend stands on.
  3. 1-level drop → no LoS within X of the blockage. 2-level drop → within X×2.
Legend is 1 space back from the ledge → X = 1. With a 1-level drop, only the single ground space behind the wall is hidden; beyond it, LoS returns. (2-level drop → X×2 = 2.)

4 Level 2 → ground pp. 25–26

Shooting from Level 2 at ground level, past a Level-1 BLOCKAGE: it blocks only the single ground space immediately behind it. Everything else stays visible.

Only the space directly behind the L1 wall is hidden from the L2 shooter.

5 Cover p. 29

COVER objects don't block LoS — you can still shoot — but the target is behind cover (each affected Aim card becomes an automatic Miss) when:

  • the LoS crosses a COVER object and the target is adjacent to it; or
  • the LoS crosses an intersection of connected objects, at least one provides COVER, and the target is adjacent to it.
LoS is clear through the Half-Wall, but T is adjacent to it → in cover.

6 Remember recap

  • Center-to-center, bird's-eye. Edges/intersections of your own space are at distance 0.
  • LoS is always two-way.
  • A BLOCKAGE edge at distance 0 from the Upper space doesn't count.
  • For the X-rule, use the farthest same-level blockage edge on the line.
  • COVER (Half-Walls, Supply Bins, Respawn Beacons) never blocks LoS.
  • Auto-outcomes by Aim card: target lower = Hit · higher = Miss · behind cover = Miss (p. 29)

## 1. Game Overview (p. 2)

**Welcome to the Apex Games (flavor).** Initiated around the year 2729 on Solace, the central planet of a cluster called the Outlands, the Apex Games are an immensely popular, widely televised bloodsport owned and organized by the Mercenary Syndicate. Originally hosted in a defunct military base on the island of Kings Canyon (an overhauled continuation of an old tournament, the Thunderdome), the Games have since spanned multiple worlds.

**Game overview.** *Apex Legends: The Board Game* is a highly competitive miniatures game based on the battle-royale video game. Opposing **squads of Legends** (warriors with exceptional combat skills and abilities) airdrop onto a gradually shrinking battleground to compete in a series of matches called the Apex Games. Players use a wide variety of weapons and actions, work together with their squadmates, and pursue the ultimate goal: **eliminate the opponents and be the last ones standing.**

There are **two books**: the **Core Rules** (this book) and the **Match Settings** book. For a first game, set the Match Settings book aside — everything needed for the first match is in the Core Rules, with recommended options marked **"First game."** (p. 7)

## 3. Get Ready for Action — Setup (pp. 7–11)

> For all games after your first, use the **Match Settings** book exclusively. The setup below is the **First game** configuration. (p. 7)

### Base setup (p. 7)
1. Place the **4 Map tiles** in the center, forming a square.
2. Place the **Round board** next to the Map.
3. Place the **Round marker** on position **1** of the Round track.
4. Place the **Initiative marker** next to the Round track.
5. Place the **Aim board** along one edge of the Map, leaving room for Aim cards.
6. Shuffle all **Aim cards** into the **Aim deck**; place it face down next to the Aim board.

### Selected Game Mode — Battle Royale (pp. 7–8)
- **Squads:** 2 vs. 2 or 3 vs. 3 Legends. Players split into two squads; each squad controls an equal number of Legends. With fewer players than Legends, some players control more than one Legend. **First game:** one Legend each.
- *Rules are written as if each player controls one Legend. Controlling multiple Legends: keep each Legend's components separate and act for each individually; "each player" then means each Legend you control.* (pp. 7–8)

**Objective — Unique Eliminations (p. 8).** The game ends immediately and **your squad wins** when either:
- **Every enemy Legend has been eliminated at least once** (each enemy Legend has lost at least one of their Deathbox tokens); **or**
- **Your squad has 2 VP (2 vs. 2) / 4 VP (3 vs. 3)** at the **start of the activation of an enemy Legend that has no Deathbox tokens.** (VP: see [Life and Death](#13-life-and-death-pp-3031), p. 30.)

**Round 8 tiebreakers (in order) (p. 8):**
1. The squad with **more Victory Points** wins.
2. Still tied → among Legends who still have **both** Deathbox tokens, the one with **more Health points** grants their squad the win.
3. Still tied → **stalemate.**

### Setup — bags & decks (p. 8)
7. Place the **Entrance board** next to the Round board; place the **4 Reward tokens** beside it.
8. Put all **Common Loot tokens** (gray) **except Starting Gear** (Common Helmets, Body Shields, Knockdown Shields) into the **Common Loot bag**.
9. Put all **Rare Loot tokens** (blue) into the **Rare Loot bag**.
10. Put all **Epic Loot tokens** (purple) into the **Epic Loot bag**.
11. Place the **Loot bags** within easy reach.
12. Shuffle the **Basic Weapon cards** into the **Weapons deck** (face down, within reach).
13. Shuffle the **Supply Bin cards** into the **Supply Bin deck** (face down, next to the Weapons deck).

### Each player takes (p. 8)
- A **Legend board** in front of them.
- **3 Starting Gear tokens:** 1 Helmet, 1 Body Shield, 1 Knockdown Shield (in their designated slots).
- **2 Ammo cubes**, one on the **0** position of each Ammo track.
- A **Shield & Health points dial** near the board: set **Shield = 20** and **Health = 40**.
- **2 double-faced Deathbox / Victory Point tokens**, placed nearby with the **Deathbox** side face up.

### Selected Map — Crossroads (p. 9)
**Components needed:** 1 Building 2×2 · 3 Buildings 2×3 · 2 Buildings 2×4 · 2 Buildings 3×3 · 1 Wall (1-length) · 2 Walls (2-length) · 2 Walls (3-length) · 3 Half-Walls · 1 Rooftop Edge Half-Wall · 10 Supply Bins · 4 Respawn Beacons.

**Build order (place on the Map as shown):**
1. Level 1 Buildings (directly onto the Map)
2. Level 2 Buildings (on top of Level 1 Buildings)
3. Walls
4. Half-Walls and the Rooftop Edge Half-Wall
5. Supply Bins and Respawn Beacons

### Selected Special Feature — The Ring (p. 10)
**Components:** 4 Ring Border miniatures · 8 Ring Movement cards.
1. Shuffle the **Ring Movement cards** into a deck on the **Entrance board**. Reveal the top card and place it back on top so its symbol aligns with the matching symbol on the Entrance board.
2. Place the **4 Ring Border miniatures** on the **4 corner spaces** of the Map, facing inward.

### Initiative and Legend Draft (pp. 10–11)
One squad has the **initiative** (acts first on the Map); the other squad drafts a Legend first. **The initiative never changes during the game.**

**First game:** Each squad draws 1 Aim card; higher value becomes the **Red squad** (redraw on a tie); reshuffle the Aim cards.
1. **Red squad** plays **Bangalore + Wraith** (2v2) or **Bangalore + Wraith + Lifeline** (3v3).
2. **Blue squad** plays **Gibraltar + Bloodhound** (2v2) or **Gibraltar + Bloodhound + Pathfinder** (3v3).
3. Freely allocate Legends to players within each squad; each player takes their **Draft card** face up.
4. Place the **Initiative marker** on the **leftmost Red-squad turn space** of the Initiative track.
5. **Skip the general draft paragraphs** and proceed to Legend Setup.

**General draft (non-first-game), for reference (p. 11):** Each squad draws 1 Aim card; higher value chooses its color — **Red** enters the Map first and always has initiative; **Blue** makes the first pick in the Legend draft (and places on the Entrance board first when applicable). Place the Initiative marker on the leftmost Red turn space, spread all Legend Draft cards face up, and pick alternately (Blue squad selects a player who picks first), returning unused Draft cards to the box. *Note: Legends may instead be assigned at random.*

### Legend Setup (p. 11)
Each player takes their squad-color **Squad identifier**, a **Help card**, their **Legend info sheet**, and (if listed for the mode) a **Player screen**, plus all components listed on the Legend info sheet. Arrange next to the Legend board:
1. Legend info sheet
2. Help card
3. Legend miniature + Squad identifier (attach the mini to the identifier)
4. Ability miniatures
5. Ability cards (face up)
6. Fast cards (shuffle the **Fast deck**, face down, in its slot)
7. Markers and tokens (abilities, cooldowns, etc.)
8. Player screen (on the Legend board, hiding the Backpack from opponents) — **First game: use Player screens.**

Miniatures, markers, and tokens form your Legend's **supply**.

## 4. Game Flow — the Three Stages (p. 12)

Each game is played in three consecutive stages:
1. **Stage 1 — Legend Loadout:** equip and cooperate with squadmates to optimize your squad's assets.
2. **Stage 2 — Map Drop:** enter the Map, seeking positional advantage and extra loot.
3. **Stage 3 — Final Encounter:** fight your opponents to achieve the victory conditions. Played in **up to 8 Rounds** — the core of the game.

---

## 5. Stage 1: Legend Loadout (pp. 12–13)

> Follow the Match Settings book for the chosen mode. **First game** rules below. (p. 12)

Each Legend forms a **Loot Pool** by drawing:
- **4 Weapon cards** from the top of the Weapons deck
- **4 random Common Loot tokens** from the Common bag
- **4 random Rare Loot tokens** from the Rare bag

During this stage **all players act simultaneously.** Each Legend may **freely give** any number of Weapon cards and Loot tokens from their Loot Pool to other Legends **in their squad.**

Then each Legend must **equip 2 Weapon cards** (one in each Weapon slot to the right of the board), and:
1. Set each weapon's **Ammo marker** to the Ammo value in the **top-right corner** of that Weapon card.
2. Place Loot tokens in **Backpack slots, Gear slots, and on Weapon cards** as appropriate, up to capacity limits (see [Handling Loot, Gear, and Weapons](#6-handling-loot-gear-and-weapons-pp-1213)).
3. **Remove all remaining Loot tokens and Weapon cards** in the Loot Pools **from the game.**

## 6. Handling Loot, Gear, and Weapons (pp. 12–13)

Use your **Legend board** to manage Loot tokens, Weapons, and Feat cards. Board areas (p. 12):
1. **Gear slots**
2. **Backpack slots**
3. **Ammo tracks** and **Ammo markers**
4. **Weapon card slots**
5. **Feat slots** (Feat deck) and **Feat discard pile slot**

### Loot tokens (p. 13)
Loot tokens restore health, increase protection, improve weapons, and more. They come in four **rarity colors** — **Common (gray) < Rare (blue) < Epic (purple) < Legendary (gold)** — and four **types** by use (full data in the [Database, p. 39+](#20-loot-tokens-database-pp-4143)):
- **Gear** — protect yourself. Placed in **Gear slots** (never in the Backpack).
- **Attachment** — enhance weapons. Placed in a **Backpack slot** *or* a **Weapon attachment slot**.
- **Grenade** — cause damage (single-use). Placed in a **Backpack slot**.
- **Consumable** — positively impact your Legend (single-use). Placed in a **Backpack slot**.

> **"Items"** = Grenades and Consumables.

When a Legend **obtains** a Loot token, they may:
- **Keep it** (in the designated board/weapon slot), or
- **Drop it** onto the Map, on their space or a **neighboring** space (see [Contiguity](#10-contiguity-pp-2021), p. 20). No limit to tokens on a space.

> When your Legend is **on the Map** and you obtain a Loot token, you either **keep** or **drop** it — it is **never discarded.**

### Gear (p. 13)
The board's upper area has slots for **3 Gear types**, by symbol: **Helmet, Body Shield, Knockdown Shield.** You may have **only 1 token of each type** at a time. You start fully equipped with **Common Gear** tokens.

When you obtain a **new Gear token**, choose: **Keep it** (dropping the existing same-type token) or **Drop it.**

> **Body Shield and Knockdown Shield tokens are double-sided** (except Common ones). When you **drag/pick one up from the Map, do not flip it** (an "Exhausted" Knockdown Shield is placed Exhausted-side up). When you obtain a two-sided Shield token from elsewhere (Loot Bag, Supply Bin, etc.), keep/drop it **front-side up.**

### Backpack (p. 13)
The **8 lower slots** of the board. Holds **Attachments, Grenades, and Consumables.** **Each Backpack slot holds only 1 Loot token.** If you want to keep a token and have no free slot, you may **drop a Backpack token** to make room.

### Weapons and Ammo (p. 13)
Weapon cards go in the **two slots** to the right of the board. **Each slot holds 1 Weapon card** (max 2 weapons equipped at once).

- To fit an Attachment when its weapon slot is occupied, either **drop** the existing Attachment or move it to an **empty Backpack slot.**
- Track each weapon's **Ammo** on the track to its left: **0 (unloaded) to 5 (fully loaded).** Move up when gaining, down when spending; never below 0 or above 5.

When you obtain Weapon card(s), choose for **each** card:
- **Replace a Weapon:** swap the new card into a slot. **Same Ammo type** → **add** the new card's Ammo value to the current Ammo marker; **different type** → **set** the marker to the new card's value. Then **discard** the replaced card.
- **Take Ammo** from the new weapon: if any equipped weapon shares its Ammo type, distribute the new Ammo freely among weapons of that type.
- **Discard** the new weapon.

Discarded Weapon cards are **removed from the game.** Attachments on a discarded weapon may be moved to another Weapon card slot (see [Attachments, p. 27](#attachments-p-27)), placed in the Backpack, or dropped.

### Feat card slots (p. 13)
Two slots on the **left** of the board: the **top** holds the **Feat deck** (face down); the **bottom** holds **discarded Feat cards** (face up).

## 7. Stage 2: Map Drop (pp. 13–14)

> Follow the Match Settings book for the chosen mode. **First game** rules below. (p. 13)

**Prepare:**
1. Randomly place **1 Reward token** next to each edge of the Entrance board.
2. Draw **1 Epic Loot token** and place it next to one edge of the Entrance board; repeat **clockwise** so there is an Epic Loot token next to each of the 4 edges.
3. Each Legend draws **3 Feat cards** from their Feat deck into hand.

**Then, in two phases** *(the example on p. 14 clarifies the order):*

**Phase A — Place at the Entrance board.** Starting with the **Blue squad** and alternating until all Legends are placed next to the Entrance board:
- The squad chooses 1 of their Legends and places its mini next to an **edge** of the Entrance board (an edge may be chosen by more than one Legend).
- That Legend takes the **Epic Loot token** next to that edge (if any) and **keeps or drops** it (see [Handling Loot…, p. 11/13](#6-handling-loot-gear-and-weapons-pp-1213)). **Tokens dropped during this step are removed from the game.**

**Phase B — Enter the Map.** Starting with the **Red squad** (which enters first) and alternating:
- The squad chooses 1 of its Legends on the Entrance board and places its mini **outside the Map, next to a space on the corresponding edge** of the Map (e.g., a Legend on the Entrance board's left edge enters from the Map's left edge). The Legend must be able to enter from that space and is considered to be at **level 0.**
- The Legend **resolves the Reward token** next to that edge of the Entrance board (**do not take** the Reward token).

Finally, **remove all remaining Epic Loot tokens and all Reward tokens from the game.**

⚠ **[Source note:** the printed numbered list reuses "1a/1b" for both phases; the two-phase reading above matches the worked example on p. 14.**]**

**Reward tokens** (each offers one):
- **Move 4**
- **Move 2 + Rare Loot**
- **Move 3 + Common Loot**
- **Move 3 + 1 Ammo**

**Resolving rewards:**
- **Move X:** perform a Move action with X MP (see [Move, p. 17](#9-actions-move-pp-1720)). The **first space entered must be on the Map**; thereafter you can never exit the Map. **Resolve the Move before any other reward.**
- **Rare / Common Loot:** draw a token from the matching bag; keep or drop.
- **Ammo:** move one weapon's Ammo marker **1 space up.**

**Example (p. 14):** Blue places Gibraltar (bottom edge; takes a Sniper Stock Epic token into his Backpack); Red places Wraith (top); Blue places Bloodhound (left); Red places Bangalore (right) — each takes the Epic token at its edge. Then Red enters first with Bangalore, who resolves her edge's Reward (Move 3 + Common Loot): she moves up onto a level 1 Building (3 MP), then draws a Syringe; her Backpack being full, she drops a Shield Cell on a neighboring space. Entry then alternates until all Legends are on the Map.

## 8. Stage 3: Final Encounter (pp. 14–17)

In Stage 3, Legends perform actions on the Map seeking victory. Played in **up to 8 Rounds.** Each Round, each squad takes a **Turn** (Red first); after both turns, a **Transition phase** prepares the next round. Tracked on the **Round board** (p. 14): **A** Round track · **B** Round marker · **C** Initiative track · **D** Initiative marker.

### Round structure (pp. 14–15)
- **Red squad's Turn:** resolve effects below the Initiative marker → each Legend (in any order) activates → advance the Initiative marker.
- **Blue squad's Turn:** same steps.
- **Transition phase.**

### Turns — step 1: Resolve Initiative Marker Effects (p. 15)
Resolve the effects of any **tokens/miniatures below the Initiative marker** (e.g., Ability tokens placed in earlier rounds — see [Ability cards, p. 35](#ability-cards-pp-3536)). With multiple, resolve **one at a time, top to bottom.**

> **Example (p. 15):** After Red's turn, the marker advances onto Blue's turn space. Two tokens sit below it, resolved top→bottom: first Bangalore's **Rolling Thunder** explodes (deals damage); then Gibraltar's **Dome of Protection** ceases. *Order matters:* if reversed, the Dome would cease before the explosion and provide no protection.

### Turns — step 2: Legend Activation (p. 15)
The squad activates each Legend **once**, in any order; a Legend must finish before the next activates. An active Legend spends **up to 2 Activation Points (AP)**. Each action is fully resolved before choosing the next (unless stated otherwise). Actions cost **1 AP, 2 AP, or are Free (0 AP).** If you lack the AP for an action, you can't perform it (e.g., with 2 AP you cannot do a 1-AP action **and then** a 2-AP action). A Legend on the Map may also **pass.**

**A. Standalone & paired basic actions** — available to every Legend, **1 AP each.** You may perform the **same action twice** per activation (2 AP) unless noted:
- **Move 2**
- **Interact and Move 1**
- **Shoot** *(only once per activation)*
- **Use and Move 1**
- **Melee Attack and Move 1**
- **Destroy 2 and Move 1**
- **Respawn** *(only when eliminated)*

> **Note:** When actions are joined by **"and"**, perform any, some, or all of them **in the order you choose.**

**B. Card actions, Instants, and Reactions** (p. 15) — Ability, Feat, and certain Weapon cards grant actions; pay the cost shown (Free / 1 AP / 2 AP).
- **Ability card** actions are unique per Legend, **never discarded** (stay by the board all game).
- **Feat card** actions are unique, **single-use**; after use, the card goes to the **Feat discard pile.**
- **Weapon card** actions require the weapon to be **equipped.**
- `INSTANT` actions can be taken during **any Legend's** activation (see [Cards, p. 34](#18-cards-pp-3440)).
- `REACTION` actions can be taken during an **enemy Shoot action** (see [Cards, p. 34](#18-cards-pp-3440)).

**C. Rush** (p. 15) — `RUSH` `MOVE 1 / +1 MP`: **once per activation**, with **no AP cost**, **discard a Feat card** from your hand to either perform a **Move 1** action or gain **+1 MP** on a Move action. The Feat card's normal effect is **disregarded**; place it on the Feat discard pile.

**D. Dropping and Picking up Loot tokens** (p. 16) — **before or after each of your actions** (basic, paired, free, or special), at **no AP cost**:
- **Drop** any number of Backpack tokens onto **your space and/or neighboring spaces** (any combination; no per-space limit).
- **Pick up** any number of tokens from **your space and/or adjacent spaces** (any combination).

> **Note:** Outside its activation, a Legend may only use **Reactions and Instants** from its cards. (p. 16)
> **Note:** An eliminated Legend **not on the Map** **cannot** perform any actions (including Reactions/Instants) during anyone's activation. They must **start their next activation with a Respawn** (1 AP), then may spend remaining AP. (p. 16)

### Precedence rules (p. 16)
- Resolve **multiple cards** in the **order they were played.**
- Conflicts: **Match Settings overrule Core Rules**; **Card instructions overrule both Match Settings and Core Rules.**

### Turns — step 3: Advance the Initiative marker (p. 16)
After all a squad's Legends have activated, advance the marker **1 position**:
- Red squad → advance to the **Blue squad** turn space.
- Blue squad → advance to the **Transition phase** area, then resolve the Transition phase.

### Transition phase (pp. 16–17)
Prepares the next round, in order:
1. **(Ring/Heat Shields only)** Deal **`HEALTH` damage** to each Legend **outside the Ring limits** — this damage **bypasses the Shield and reduces Health directly.** The amount depends on the **Round marker position** (shown at the bottom of the Round track) — see [Life and Death, p. 30](#13-life-and-death-pp-3031). *Skip if not using the Ring/Heat Shields.*
2. Each Legend **draws 1 Feat card** (no hand limit). If your Feat deck is empty, draw nothing.
3. Move all Ability tokens on the **Cooldown track 1 space left.** A token moved **off** the track has finished cooldown: return it to its owner, next to its Ability card (see [Cooldown, p. 38](#cooldown-pp-3839)).
4. **Advance the Round marker 1 space right** and resolve the new position:
   - **Trophy icon:** the game **ends**; determine the result per the Game Mode.
   - **Square (dotted) space:** when using **Ring/Heat Shields**, resolve each icon on that space; **always advance the Round marker 1 more space** afterward (regardless of features used).
   - **Diamond space (p. 17):** if any tokens are below the new Round-marker position, resolve their effects.
   - **Ring movement icon:** move the Ring Border miniatures per the revealed Ring Movement card (the card represents the Map; each edge maps to the matching Map edge). Move the 2 Ring Borders on each edge in the arrow's direction by the number of spaces shown.
   - **Ring shuffle icon:** shuffle the current Ring Movement card back into the deck, reveal a new top card, and align its symbol to the Entrance board (this shows the next movement pattern).
5. **Flip all Legend Draft cards face up.**
6. **Advance the Initiative marker** to the next squad's turn space (follow the arrows).

> **Ring movement example (p. 17):** Move the 2 top Ring Borders 2 spaces down; move the 2 left Ring Borders 2 spaces right; do not move the bottom-right Ring Border. The area inside the frame is **"inside the ring limits"** (the safe zone).

## 9. Actions: Move (pp. 17–20)

Movement uses **Movement Points (MP)** — the number after "Move" (e.g., **Move 2** = 2 MP). You may spend **all, some, or none** of the available MP. Unused MP are **lost** when the action ends. **Each Move action is resolved individually** (you cannot combine two Move 1 actions into a Move 2). Some special/free actions enable or modify a Move.

### The Map & the grid (pp. 17–18)
The Map is the arena: terrain (Buildings, Walls, Rocks, Trees) and objects (Supply Bins, Respawn Beacons). Your Legend is its miniature. The grid defines:
- **Spaces** — each grid square
- **Edges** — the borderlines of a square
- **Intersections** — the corners of a square

Spaces, edges, and intersections may be **Free** or **Occupied** depending on components placed there; occupied features affect placement and movement. (Full per-object data: Object Reference sheet / Legend info sheet.)

### Levels (p. 18)
Vertical position is measured in **levels** from the ground (**level 0**) up. Some components have a `LEVEL`, setting the level of map areas; **levels add for stacked components**. On Crossroads, **Buildings and Walls are `LEVEL` components** (all other Crossroads components have no level for gameplay). Two stacked Buildings = a **Level 2** object; a Wall on a Level 1 Building = a **Level 2** object. *(Miniature attributes are printed as tags such as `LEVEL`; see the Object Reference / Legend info sheets.)*

### Buildings (p. 18)
Each Building = **walls + rooftop.** Building Walls are `BLOCKAGE` (may block LoS) and `CONNECTIBLE` (connect with other `CONNECTIBLE`s at a shared intersection). **Rooftop spaces** work like any other spaces and are **one level higher** than the Building's base; their corners are **free intersections distinct** from those on other levels.

### Movement basics (p. 18)
Move from space to space — **orthogonally or diagonally**, same level or across levels — checking conditions **1 space at a time.** Spend MP per space entered; you may not enter a space without enough MP.

Object attributes impose restrictions:
- `IMPASSABLE` — **prevents** movement.
- `OBSTACLE` — adds **extra cost** to movement.

On Crossroads: **Respawn Beacons are the only `IMPASSABLE` objects**; **Walls, Half-Walls, and Supply Bins are `OBSTACLE`s.**

Movement rules:
- **Map borders:** you cannot move off the Map.
- **Free spaces:** always enterable. A free space may hold components that **don't `OCCUPY SPACE`** (e.g., Loot tokens).
- **Occupied spaces:** you may **enter but not end** on a space with a miniature that has `OCCUPY SPACE` (e.g., a Legend); you need enough MP to enter **and pass through.**
- **Impassable spaces:** you cannot enter spaces occupied by `IMPASSABLE` objects.
- **Across levels:** you can **never move directly from level 0 to level 2**; you can never move across a `LEVEL` component placed **one or more levels higher** than your Legend; you **may move down any number of levels.**
- **Connected components:** Buildings/`CONNECTIBLE` objects sharing an intersection are **connected** (90° or 180°). Crossing a shared intersection: **consider both components' effects**; use the one with the **lowest movement restriction**; you **cannot** cross if **both** prevent movement. Shared intersections of connected components on **lower levels** than your Legend **do not influence movement.**

### Movement cost (p. 19)
- **1 MP** per space entered.
- **+1 MP** to cross an `OBSTACLE` on the **same level or one level up.**
- **+1 MP** to cross an **intersection of connected components** on the same level or one level up.
- **+1 MP** to move **one level up** (0→1 or 1→2).
- **+0 MP** to move **down.**

**Same-level examples (p. 19):** Stopping next to a Respawn Beacon (1 MP; cannot enter it — `IMPASSABLE`). Moving past a Supply Bin would cost 2 MP for that space (1 + 1 for the `OBSTACLE`). Moving **across another Legend's** space costs no extra (1 MP per space). Crossing a Building/Half-Wall shared intersection costs 2 MP (1 + 1 for the Half-Wall).

**Up/down examples (pp. 19–20):**
- A Level 2 Building and a Wall are connected; the Building **prevents** the move (can't go 2 levels up), but the Wall imposes the **lesser** restriction (+1), so the diagonal move costs **2 MP** (1 + 1 across the Wall).
- Crossing a **level 1 shared intersection** on top of two connected Buildings costs **2 MP**; if both were Level 2, the move would be impossible (intersection 2 levels above the Legend).
- Going **around** connected objects (crossing a shared intersection but **no `OBSTACLE`**) costs no extra.
- **Moving down** any number of levels costs only **1 MP** for entering the new space; moving **1 level up** costs **2 MP**; up **1 level + across a Half-Wall** costs **3 MP** (so with only 2 MP it's not possible).
- Moving **down across** a connected-object intersection on your level has **no extra cost** (1 MP), because you are moving downward.

**Move combined with another basic action (p. 20):** e.g., "Interact and Move 1" — perform the two parts in either order (Interact with an adjacent Supply Bin first, then move; or move adjacent first, then Interact). You cannot move **across** the Supply Bin cheaply (it's an `OBSTACLE`).

**Move enhanced with Rush (p. 20):** Choosing **Move 2**, then performing **Rush** (discard a Feat, **+1 MP**) gives **3 MP** to spend on that Move.

## 10. Contiguity (pp. 20–21)

Keywords describing the relationship between nearby spaces in the 3D map. For a reference space **X**:

- **Distance 1 from X:** all spaces **touching X** orthogonally **and** diagonally — **regardless of level and any obstacles in between.** (p. 20)
- **Adjacent to X:** spaces at distance 1 that are on the **same level** as X **and** have **no obstacles in between** (a space is not adjacent to itself). A space separated from X by an obstacle (e.g., a Half-Wall) or on a different level is **not** adjacent. (pp. 20–21)
- **Neighboring X:** spaces at distance 1, on the **same or lower** level, with **no obstacles in between.** A higher-level space is **not** neighboring; a space blocked by an obstacle is **not** neighboring. (p. 21)

> All objects considered obstacles carry the `OBSTACLE` tag. (p. 21)

**Edges and intersections** also use adjacency/neighboring (p. 21):
- **Edges:** consider the **2 spaces sharing the edge.** *Your Legend is adjacent to a Building Wall only if on a space at the **same level** as the **base** of that Building Wall.*
- **Intersections:** consider the **4 spaces sharing the intersection.** Spaces are adjacent to an intersection only if on the **same level** as it; intersections on **different levels are treated as separate.**

## 11. Interact (pp. 21–22)

The Interact action lets you do one or more of the following, **once each, in any order:**
- **Interact with 1 `INTERACTIVE` object adjacent** to your Legend (the Object Reference sheet lists which objects are `INTERACTIVE`).
- **Fit** any number of Attachments **from your Backpack** onto your Weapon.
- **Return** any number of weapon-fitted Attachments to your Backpack.

Backpack capacity and attachment-fitting conditions apply (see [Handling Loot…, p. 11/13](#6-handling-loot-gear-and-weapons-pp-1213) and [Attachments, p. 27](#attachments-p-27)).

### Interact with a closed Supply Bin (loot it) (p. 21)
1. Reveal **1 card** from the **Supply Bin deck.**
2. Randomly draw the **indicated Loot tokens** from the matching bags; place them near your board.
3. Draw **2 Weapon cards**; choose **one** to keep (set it near your board) and **remove the other** from the game.
4. **Keep or drop** each Loot token; for the chosen Weapon, pick one: **Replace** an existing weapon, **Take the Ammo** from it, or **Discard** it (see [Weapons and Ammo, p. 12/13](#weapons-and-ammo-p-13)).
5. Place an **opened marker** on the Supply Bin (it can no longer be looted) and **remove the Supply Bin card** from the game.

> **Example (p. 21):** Gibraltar (adjacent to a Supply Bin) spends 1 AP for "Interact and Move 1," Interacting first: reveals the top Supply Bin card, draws the indicated tokens (1 Epic, 1 Rare, 1 Common), draws 2 Weapon cards and keeps one, resolves the Weapon and tokens, then may still Move 1.

### Other interactive objects (p. 22)
- **Legend-specific objects:** some abilities place new `INTERACTIVE` objects — see the Legend's Ability card / info sheet.
- **Mode-/Map-/Feature-specific objects:** see the Match Settings book and Object Reference sheet.

## 12. Shoot (pp. 22–30)

Shooting at enemy Legends is your primary way to deal damage. **You may perform a Shoot action only once per activation** — whether it comes from a basic, Free, or Special action.

The shooting Legend is the **Shooter**; the one shot at is the **Target.** Procedure, in order (p. 22):
1. **Aim** (p. 23): a) choose a **Target in LoS**; b) choose a **weapon and spend 1 Ammo**; c) determine the **To-Hit Difficulty**; d) *(optional)* play **1 `ENHANCE—SHOOT`** card.
2. **Enemy Reaction — Before Firing** (p. 28–29): the Target may play 1 `REACTION` `BEFORE FIRING`; each of the Target's squadmates may play 1 `REACTION` `SQUAD` `BEFORE FIRING`.
3. **Fire** (pp. 28–30): a) **reveal Aim cards**; b) **determine Hits and Headshots**; c) **deal Damage.**
4. **Enemy Reaction — After Firing** (p. 30): the Target may play 1 `REACTION` `AFTER FIRING`; each squadmate may play 1 `REACTION` `SQUAD` `AFTER FIRING`.
5. **Reset the Aim deck** (p. 30): shuffle all used Aim cards back in.

### Distance Measurement (p. 22)
Distance = the **smallest number of spaces** between start and end points (**including** the end space). **Level, obstacles, and objects don't affect distance.** A space's own edges and intersections are at **distance 0** from it.
- **Space-to-space:** count spaces between (level difference ignored).
- **Space-to-edge:** an edge is shared by 2 spaces — use the **closer** one.
- **Space-to-intersection:** an intersection is shared by 4 spaces — use the **closest** one.

### 1a. Choose a Target in Line of Sight (pp. 23–26)
Draw an imaginary **LoS line** from the **center** of the Shooter's space to the **center** of the Target's space (or target intersection). You may only shoot targets in an **unblocked LoS.**

**LoS basics (p. 23):** LoS exists if there's no **effective `BLOCKAGE`** along the line between **source** and **target.** An effective `BLOCKAGE` **blocks LoS both ways** (no one-way sight). On Crossroads, `BLOCKAGE`s are **Building Walls** and standalone **Walls**; some Legends can place `BLOCKAGE` miniatures. Objects providing `COVER` (Half-Walls, Supply Bins) **do not block LoS** (but may affect the Shoot result).

General `BLOCKAGE` rules (p. 23):
- **Connected components:** if the LoS crosses a shared intersection and **both** components are `BLOCKAGE`, assess their effect (otherwise no effect).
- **45° Rule:** a `BLOCKAGE` touched **only at its corner** has **no effect** on LoS.
- A `BLOCKAGE` **higher than both source and target blocks** the LoS.

> **Examples (p. 23):** Wraith doesn't see Bloodhound (LoS crosses a Wall `BLOCKAGE`); Wraith and Gibraltar **do** see each other (LoS only touches the Wall's corner — 45° Rule). Gibraltar sees Bangalore over a Half-Wall (not a `BLOCKAGE`). Bloodhound on a higher level can't see level-0 targets beyond a connected Building+Wall that is higher than source and those targets.

**Specific cases — when the `BLOCKAGE` is not higher than both source and target (pp. 24–26).** Of the source and target, the higher one is the **Upper space**; the other is the **Lower space.** If none of the cases below produce a block, source and target **see each other.**

**Upper space with a `BLOCKAGE` at the same level (p. 24):**
1. From the Upper space, find the distance to the **farthest** `BLOCKAGE` **edge** along the LoS **at the same level.** If that distance is **0** (the edge is an edge of the Upper space), it **does not count** as a `BLOCKAGE` — there is LoS.
2. Extend an imaginary line along that `BLOCKAGE` edge across the Map. The **shortest orthogonal distance** from the Upper space to that line is **X.**
3. Level difference:
   - **1-level difference:** LoS is blocked to all spaces between the `BLOCKAGE` and the Lower space **within X** of the `BLOCKAGE`.
   - **2-level difference:** LoS is blocked to all such spaces **within X×2** of the `BLOCKAGE`.

> **Worked examples (pp. 24–25):**
> - **Gibraltar (upper) ↔ Wraith:** only `BLOCKAGE` is the level-1 Building edge under Gibraltar; **X = 1**, 1-level difference → no LoS within 1 space of the `BLOCKAGE`; Wraith is outside → **LoS exists.**
> - **Bloodhound ↔ Gibraltar (upper):** two level-1 `BLOCKAGE`s; the **farther** one (a Wall near Bloodhound) is used; **X = 3**, 1-level difference → Bloodhound lies within 3 → **LoS blocked.**
> - **Bangalore (on the edge, distance 0) ↔ Gibraltar:** the edge doesn't count; no other `BLOCKAGE` → **clear LoS.**
> - **Bangalore ↔ Wraith:** Wall near Wraith, **X = 2**, 1-level difference → Wraith within 2 → **blocked.**
> - **Wraith (upper, level 2) ↔ Gibraltar:** level-2 Building edge, **X = 1**, 1-level difference → blocked within 1 space; Gibraltar outside → **LoS exists.**
> - **Wraith (level 2) ↔ Bloodhound:** **X = 1**, **2-level** difference → blocked within **1×2 = 2** spaces → **blocked.**
> - **Intersection targets:** the same X / area logic applies when targeting an intersection (e.g., placing a Dome of Protection): an intersection inside the blocked area is not in LoS; one outside is.

**LoS from level 2 to ground level with a `BLOCKAGE` on level 1 (pp. 25–26):** Only the **ground-level space immediately behind** the level-1 `BLOCKAGE` is blocked; **no other spaces** are blocked. *(Note: `BLOCKAGE` edges at distance 0 from the Upper space don't count.)*

> **Examples (p. 26):** From a level-2 Upper space, a level-1 Wall blocks **only the space directly behind it** (so a Legend not directly behind it remains visible). A level-2 Building edge at distance 0 doesn't count; a level-1 Building still blocks only the space immediately behind it.

### 1b. Choose a Weapon and spend 1 Ammo (p. 26)
Choose a weapon **with Ammo** (its marker isn't on **0**). Slide that weapon's Ammo marker **1 space down** — **regardless of the weapon's Fire Rate.**

### Weapons (pp. 26–27)
Equipped weapons are Weapon cards in your board's Weapon slots. **Weapon card anatomy** (callouts 1–11):
1. **Name and Weapon type**
2. **Weapon ID**
3. **Ammo** — icon = Ammo type; number = initial Ammo in the weapon
4. **Optimal Range** — the distance interval where the weapon is most effective (firing outside it worsens To-Hit Difficulty)
5. **To-Hit Difficulty** — how hard to score a Hit (higher = harder)
6. **Fire Rate** — number of cards you reveal on the Aim board during the Shoot action
7. **Regular Damage** — damage per scored Hit
8. **Headshot Damage** — **additional** damage per scored Headshot
9. **Actions and effects** — extra actions/effects; follow the card
10. **Attachment slots** — which Attachments may be fitted (some weapons have printed Attachments)
11. **Preinstalled Hop-Up** — a modified weapon vs. the base model: **△ = improvement**, **▽ = downgrade**; may add a passive effect (yellow `PASSIVE` box)

> **Example weapon — Longbow DMR (Sniper, ID W-34):** Optimal Range **6+**, To-Hit Difficulty **55+**, Fire Rate **2**, Regular Damage **10**, Headshot Damage **18**, Ammo **2** (Sniper type), `PASSIVE`: **"Apply RECOIL after each shot."** Attachment slots: Barrel Stabilizer, Extended Magazine, Optics, Sniper Stock. *(Values verified from the card on p. 26.)*

**Actions and effects (p. 27):**
- A **Passive effect** usually applies whenever the weapon is used in a Shoot. Some require a condition — **check it once, before revealing any Aim cards.**
- A **Special action** is performed during your activation for the listed AP. If a Special action lets you Shoot and you choose to, you **can do nothing else but Shoot** with that action. Instructions joined by "and" may be done in any order.

> **Note:** Resolving multiple cards in a Shoot — the in-use weapon's **Passive effects are considered to have started at the beginning of the action**; other cards count as played afterward. (p. 27)

#### Attachments (p. 27)
Four types:
- **Barrel Stabilizers** — increase Stability (easier to Hit; shift Aim-board start left).
- **Extended Magazines / Shotgun Bolts** — increase **Fire Rate.**
- **Optics** — decrease **To-Hit Difficulty** for specified ranges.
- **Stocks** — increase **Damage** of scored Hits.

Fit an Attachment only if it **matches the weapon's attachment-slot icon.** Slots may add requirements:
- **Ammo Type:** only Attachments showing that Ammo-type icon fit.
- **Node System:** the Attachment's node must match **at least one** white node on the slot.

> **Printed Attachments:** some weapons have Attachments printed on the card — they're **active and cannot be transferred/discarded.** You may place an Attachment token **on top** of a printed one (if requirements are met); the printed one is then inactive until you remove the token, after which it reactivates. (p. 27)

### 1c. Determine the To-Hit Difficulty (p. 27)
Start from the weapon's To-Hit Difficulty and apply **all** applicable modifiers (cumulative):
- **Range modifier:** for **each space outside** the Optimal Range (above or below), **+10** To-Hit Difficulty.
- **Optics modifier:** if equipped with an Optics Attachment and the target is within the optic's range, **decrease** To-Hit Difficulty by the optic's value.
- **Other modifiers:** any other card/component effects.

> **Example (p. 28):** Shooting the **C.A.R. SMG** (base To-Hit **50**): target at range 2 = one space below optimal → **+10**; fitted optics (range 1–2) → **−5**. Modified To-Hit = **50 + 10 − 5 = 55** (need a 55+ result to Hit).

### 1d. (Optional) Play 1 Enhance—Shoot card (p. 28)
You may play **1** `ENHANCE—SHOOT` card; apply its effects as described. **Limit 1** per Shoot.
> Instant cards with a "Play as Enhance—Shoot" effect **count toward** that limit.

### 2. Enemy Reaction — Before Firing (pp. 28–29)
Each Legend can react to **each** Shoot action if it has the cards (so a Legend can react to multiple Shoots in a round). The **Target** plays **1** `REACTION` card (Ability or Feat) with **'Before Firing'** (may also have **'Squad'**). Each **squadmate** of the Target plays **1** card having **both 'Before Firing' and 'Squad'.** Resolve played Reactions **before** continuing the Shoot; with multiples, the **targeted squad** chooses the order. Fast Reaction cards go to the Feat discard pile. *Some effects may not resolve immediately* — see [Cards, p. 34](#18-cards-pp-3440).

### 3. Fire
#### 3a. Reveal Aim cards (pp. 28–29)
Reveal Aim cards equal to your weapon's **Fire Rate** (plus any extra effects). **Every drawn Aim card must be placed in an Aim-board slot**; each slot has a **Stability modifier.** Cards that would go **beyond the rightmost slot are placed in that slot instead.**

**Aim board slot modifiers, left → right: `+5, 0, −5, −10, −10, −15`.** *(Verified on p. 28.)*

Placement details: the **first card** goes in the slot indicated by Fire Rate (a **Barrel Stabilizer** shifts the start left). **Recoil** shifts subsequent cards further right (see [Recoil, p. 39](#19-the-database--terminology-pp-3940)).

> **Example (pp. 28–29):** Shooting the **Longbow** at a target **6 spaces** away and **at a lower level**; final To-Hit **55+** (no modifiers). Use **3 Aim-board slots** (the weapon's 2 + 1 from an **Extended Magazine**). With a Barrel Stabilizer, the first card sits one slot left of the To-Hit value; the Longbow's Recoil Passive pushes each subsequent card further right.

#### 3b. Determine Hits and Headshots (p. 29)
Check each revealed Aim card **one at a time** (even multiple in one slot), **left slot → right.** First count Hits, then Headshots.

**Regular Hits:**
1. Count **Semi-Hit** icons across all revealed Aim cards: **every 2 Semi-Hit icons = 1 Hit** (e.g., 3 Semi-Hits → 1 Hit).
2. Check each card for **automatic-outcome icons** (card value ignored here):
   - **Auto-Hit** (target **lower** level than Shooter) → this card **scores a Hit.**
   - **Auto-Miss** (target **higher** level) → this card **Misses.**
   - **Cover Auto-Miss** (target **behind cover**) → this card **Misses.**
3. Otherwise, **add the Aim card value + its slot's Stability modifier**; if the result **≥ modified To-Hit Difficulty**, it's a **Hit.**

*(Tip: to keep track of total Hits, **slide down the Aim cards that missed**.)*
> Some Feat/Ability cards say to treat Aim cards with a certain icon as **not having** that icon — all effects of that icon are then ignored. (p. 29)

**Cover (pp. 29).** Objects with the `COVER` attribute provide cover (on Crossroads: **Half-Walls, Respawn Beacons, Supply Bins**). The Target is **behind cover** when:
- the LoS **crosses a `COVER` object** and the Target is **adjacent** to it; **or**
- the LoS **crosses an intersection of connected objects**, **at least one** provides `COVER`, and the Target is **adjacent** to that object.

> **Example (p. 29):** Gibraltar targets Bangalore; the LoS crosses a connected Supply Bin + Half-Wall intersection; the Half-Wall has `COVER` and Bangalore is adjacent → **Bangalore is in cover.**

**Headshots (p. 29).** Among cards that **scored a Hit** and have a **headshot icon**, compare each card's **value (ignore modifiers)** to the Target's **Helmet** protection:
- A Helmet token with a value protects against Aim cards **up to that value** — those cards **don't** score Headshots.
- Protection is **limited to the number of cards** shown on the Helmet (e.g., "up to 2 Aim cards of value ≤ 60"); **extra** qualifying cards **do** Headshot regardless of value.
> When evaluating Helmet protection, use **only the Aim card value** (ignore the Aim board's Stability modifier).

> **Example (pp. 29–30):** With modified To-Hit **55**: card 1 is an **Auto-Hit** (firing at a lower level); card 2 = 20 − 5 = 15 → **Miss**; card 3 = 70 − 10 = 60 → **Hit**, and it has a headshot icon → **Headshot.** Result: **2 Hits + 1 Headshot.** The Target's **Epic Helmet** ignores up to 2 headshots on cards **≤ 60**, but the headshot is on a **70** card → not prevented. *(If it were ≤ 60, the headshot would be prevented, leaving 2 Hits and no Headshot.)*

#### 3c. Deal Damage (p. 30)
Total damage = for each **Hit**, the weapon's **Regular Damage**; for each **Headshot**, the **additional Headshot Damage.** Apply extra effects (Attachments, Enhance-Shoot, Before-Firing Reactions). The Target subtracts the total on its **Shield & Health dial** (see [Life and Death](#13-life-and-death-pp-3031)).

> **Example (p. 30):** 2 Hits + 1 Headshot with the Longbow → **10 + 10** (Hits) **+ 14** (Headshot) **= 34**. Target's Shield 15 → 0 (absorbs 15); remaining **19** hits Health 30 → **11.**
> ⚠ **[Source note:** this example uses **14** Headshot Damage (total 34), but the Longbow **card** shows Headshot Damage **18** (which would total 38). The example appears internally inconsistent with the card; treat the **card value (18)** as authoritative for the Longbow. See [Appendix](#appendix-noted-source-inconsistencies--ambiguities).**]**

#### 4. Enemy Reaction — After Firing (p. 30)
If not eliminated, the **Target** may play **1** `REACTION` card with **'After Firing'** (may also have 'Squad'); each **squadmate** plays **1** card with **both 'After Firing' and 'Squad'** (squadmates may react **even if the Target was eliminated** during the Shoot). Resolve immediately; with multiples, the targeted squad chooses the order. Fast Reaction cards go to the Feat discard pile.

#### 5. Reset the Aim deck (p. 30)
Shuffle all used Aim cards back into the Aim deck; place it face down by the Aim board.

### Fire Rate (reference)
**Fire Rate** = the number of cards revealed on the Aim board for the Shoot (weapon stat, p. 27; used in step 3a, p. 28). It may be a **single number** (e.g., "2" = number of **slots** to use, one card each) or a **pair** (e.g., "1+3" = first number is **slots**, second number is **cards per slot**) — see [Terminology, p. 39](#19-the-database--terminology-pp-3940).

## 13. Life and Death (pp. 30–31)

Shield and Health are tracked on a **double dial.** Gains/losses adjust immediately; you may **never exceed** the limits (excess is lost).

**Shield (p. 30):**
- Starting Shield = **20** (the starting gear's protection) — the initial **Shield limit.** Damage usually hits the **Shield first.**
- Equipping a new **Body Shield** sets the **new Shield limit** to that token's value.
- Taking damage reduces Shield points.
- At **Shield 0**, the Shield no longer protects; further damage reduces **Health.**

**Health (pp. 30–31):**
- Starting Health = **40** — always the **Health limit.**
- Take Health damage when you have no Shield, your Shield is 0, or the damage bypasses the Shield.
- If damage would reduce Health to **0** and your **Knockdown Shield is active** (Deathbox-icon side up), it **automatically prevents** the amount shown on the token; apply any remaining damage, then **flip the Knockdown Shield to its 'Exhausted' side** (no longer protects).
- At **Health 0**, your Legend is **eliminated.**

> **Damage keywords (p. 31):** `SHIELD` damage impacts **only the Shield** (no Health effect, even if the Shield is destroyed). `HEALTH` damage **bypasses the Shield**, hitting Health directly.

**Elimination (p. 31).** Immediately:
1. Remove the Legend mini from the Map to your supply.
2. If the Knockdown Shield is 'Exhausted', **flip it** (active again).
3. Set Shield = **20**, Health = **40.**

Then the **enemy squad** takes your **Deathbox token, flips it to the Victory Point side**, and places it by their boards (**each VP token = 1 Victory Point**). While not on the Map you **cannot act or use cards**; return via a **Respawn** action (p. 34).

## 14. Use (pp. 31–32)

The **Use** action lets you benefit from **Items** in your Backpack: **Use a Grenade** or **Use a Consumable.**
> "Use an Item" = use **either** a Grenade **or** a Consumable. (p. 31)

### Use a Grenade (pp. 31–32)
In order: 1) intended landing space → 2) actual landing space → 3) deal damage.

**Intended landing space (p. 31):** take a Grenade token from your Backpack, **flip it**, and place it on a space **within the token's range**, facing your chosen direction. The space may be on a **different level** and may be **occupied. LoS is not required.**

**Actual landing space (p. 31):**
1. Draw the **number of Aim cards shown on the token**, one by one, **face up in draw order** beside the Aim board — **do not rotate the cards.**
2. Starting with the **first** drawn card, **relocate the Grenade 1 space** in the **direction of the card's arrow** (no arrow → no move). **Relocation ignores Buildings/Walls/components** (you're assessing aim accuracy, not actually moving the grenade). If relocated off the Map, imagine the Map extended and continue.
3. Repeat for all drawn cards; the final space is the **landing space.** If it lands **on top of an object**, it's at that **object's level** (e.g., on a Supply Drop = Level 1). If off the Map, it's at **ground level** for damage.
> When using a Grenade, consider **only the bottom section** of the Aim cards. (p. 31)

> **Example (pp. 31–32):** Gibraltar can't shoot Bangalore (Wall blocks LoS), so he throws a **Frag Grenade** at distance **5** (the grenade's max), aiming adjacent to Bangalore. Frag draws **2 Aim cards**; the grenade relocates per each card's bottom arrow in draw order and lands one space left of Bangalore.

**Dealing damage (p. 32):** the token shows the **AoE shape and damage** (which may differ per space within the area). Reduce Shield/Health of all affected Legends accordingly (see [Life and Death](#13-life-and-death-pp-3031)). Then **remove the Grenade token** and shuffle used Aim cards back.

> **Example (p. 32):** A Legend on the **center** of the AoE (red diamond) takes **20**; on **adjacent** AoE spaces, **10** (so Bangalore takes 10).

### Area of Effect (AoE) (p. 32)
Grenades and certain effects have an AoE shown on the token/card: a **source space** (red diamond) and a grid of **target spaces** (including the source); damage may vary per space.
- If the AoE source has the **`AERIAL`** tag, the **full** shown area is affected, **ignoring `BLOCKAGE`s and levels.**
- Otherwise, the AoE **does not extend past `BLOCKAGE`s** or to **levels** higher/lower than the source (use [LoS rules, p. 23](#1a-choose-a-target-in-line-of-sight-pp-2326)); the connected-components rules apply.

> **Example (p. 32):** **Rolling Thunder** has a **5 + 4**-space AoE and the `AERIAL` tag, so it affects spaces behind Walls and on different levels. Without `AERIAL`, the AoE would **not** reach spaces behind a `BLOCKAGE` (e.g., the corner Wall) or on higher levels (level-1 Buildings).

### Use a Consumable (p. 32)
Take a Consumable from your Backpack, **immediately resolve** its effect (described on the token; see [Database, p. 41+](#20-loot-tokens-database-pp-4143)), and **remove** it.
> For Shield/Health Consumables, adjust the dials by the listed values; **never exceed** the Health limit (**40**) or your Shield limit (e.g., **25** with a particular Body Shield). Excess is lost. (p. 32)

## 15. Melee Attack & Push (p. 33)

### Melee Attack
Hand-to-hand attack on **one enemy Legend in an adjacent space:**
1. Choose 1 **adjacent** enemy Legend.
2. Reveal **1 Aim card.**
3. If the value is **40+**: deal **4 damage** and **Push** the enemy **1 space away.** Otherwise the attack **fails.** If the Push causes a **Collision**, deal **4 additional damage** (see Push).
4. Shuffle the Aim card back.

### Push
- The Push value = the **maximum** number of spaces the enemy can be pushed.
- Relocate the enemy **away from you in a straight line, one space at a time**, checking for **Collision** each step; continue up to the maximum.
- A **Collision** occurs if the enemy would be pushed: **off the Map**, onto a **higher level**, onto an **occupied same-level space** (e.g., a Legend), **across occupied edges** (e.g., a Supply Bin), or **across intersections of connected components on the same level** → **do not relocate beyond this space.**
  - If pushed onto an **occupied lower-level space**, the **pushed Legend's controller** places it on a **free neighboring space** of the landing space.
- On a Collision, the enemy takes **additional damage** (4 for a Melee Attack, or the amount on the card causing the Push); the Push **ends immediately.**

> **Examples (p. 33):** (1) Aim **50** → 4 damage, push 1; landing space is lower and occupied, so the controller places Bloodhound on a free neighboring space of the Respawn Beacon, taking **+4** (Collision). (2) Aim **75** → 4 damage, push 1 blocked by a Wall → Collision: Bloodhound stays put and takes **+4.**

## 16. Destroy (pp. 33–34)

You may Destroy miniatures with the **`DESTROYABLE`** tag. The number after "Destroy" = max miniatures per action (e.g., **Destroy 2**).
1. Choose **a weapon with Ammo** *or* a **hand-to-hand** attack — with multiple targets, use the **same attack for all.**
2. Choose up to the max number of `DESTROYABLE` targets:
   - **Weapon:** all targets must be **in LoS** and within the weapon's **Optimal Range** (you **do not** spend Ammo).
   - **Hand-to-hand:** all targets must be **adjacent** to (or on) your Legend's space.
3. Resolve any effects of the targets' destruction. (p. 34)
4. Return destroyed miniatures to their owners' supplies.
5. If destroyed miniatures have Ability tokens under the Initiative track, immediately place those tokens on the **Cooldown track** (see [Cooldown, p. 38](#cooldown-pp-3839)).

## 17. Respawn (p. 34)

Once eliminated, a Legend may spend **1 AP** to **Respawn** and return to the Map. They **cannot perform any other action (or card action) before respawning.**
- Eliminated **during your activation** with AP left → you may immediately spend **1 AP** to Respawn. Otherwise, on your **next activation** you **must** Respawn **first.**
- Place your mini on a **free space adjacent to the Respawn Beacon closest to a squadmate** (tie → choose; no squadmate on the Map → any Respawn Beacon).
- After respawning, spend any remaining AP as usual.

## 18. Cards (pp. 34–40)

Feats and abilities can only be performed by **Legends on the Map.** They appear on **Feat cards** and **Ability cards** (further explained on Legend info sheets) and may carry the labels below.
> **Reminder:** even card-granted Shoots count — **you can Shoot only once per activation** (basic, Free, or Special). (p. 34)

### Free, Special, and Enhance—Shoot actions (p. 34) — **black** labels; usable **during your activation only**
- **`FREE ACTION`** — no AP; resolve the card.
- **`SPECIAL ACTION: 1 AP` / `SPECIAL ACTION: 2 AP`** — spend the exact cost; resolve.
- **`ENHANCE—SHOOT`** — played **in addition** to the named effect (e.g., a Shoot). **Only 1 Enhance card** may boost an action.

### Reactions (p. 34) — `REACTION` label; **only to react to an enemy Shoot**, always optional
- **`SQUAD`** — playable when **you or a squadmate** is targeted. Without 'Squad', only when **you** are the Target.
- **`BEFORE FIRING`** — played in the **Before Firing** step (p. 28).
- **`AFTER FIRING`** — played in the **After Firing** step (p. 30).
> Each Legend may perform **only one Before Firing and/or one After Firing** Reaction **per Shoot action** (e.g., a Before-Firing Ability Reaction blocks a Before-Firing Feat Reaction to the same Shoot).

### Instants (p. 34) — `INSTANT` label
Playable **during any Legend's activation** (yours, ally, or enemy) as long as the card's **condition is met.**
> **Example (p. 34):** When a squadmate Respawns, **Lifeline** plays **Guardian Angel** (Instant Feat): the squadmate Respawns **without spending AP** and (if it's the squadmate) **on a free space adjacent to Lifeline.** Then the Feat goes to the discard pile.

### Feat cards (p. 35)
Each Legend has a unique Feat deck. Card anatomy: 1) Legend name · 2) Feat name · 3) Type and/or Cost · 4) Feat description · 5) **Rush action** strip · 6) Flavor text.
- **Performing feats:** select a Feat from hand, resolve it, then place it **face up on the Feat discard pile.**
- **Rush:** **once per activation, no AP** — discard a Feat from hand to **perform a Move 1** *or* **add +1 MP** to one Move action (Move 1 → 2 MP, Move 2 → 3 MP, etc.). The discarded card's main effect is **ignored**; place it on the discard pile.

### Ability cards (pp. 35–36)
Each Legend has **2** Ability cards: **1 Tactical + 1 Ultimate** (with their markers/minis, allocated at draft). Anatomy: 1) Legend name · 2) Ability name + type (Tactical/Ultimate) · 3) Type and/or Cost · 4) Ability description · 5) Ability token image · 6) Cooldown time (in rounds).
- **Performing abilities:** you must have **at least one of the ability's tokens in your supply** (tokens **below the Initiative track** or **on the Cooldown track** are not available).

**Placing ability miniatures/markers on the Map (pp. 35–36):** meet **all** conditions on the card, or you can't perform the ability. If you must place a `PERSISTENT` miniature you don't have in supply, **take it from the Map** instead. Placement keywords/icons:

| Instruction | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Place on** a space/intersection | Place on **any** space/intersection, regardless of occupation. |
| **Place on** a **free** space/intersection | Place only on a space/intersection that is **not occupied.** (For connected components at **180°**, only the **outer** intersections are free; the middle is occupied. For **90°**, the shared intersection is used to place miniatures **outside** the angle.) |
| **Attach to** | Place the miniature on the **space occupied by the effect's target**; the mini is now connected to that target and they always move/relocate **together** (and stay together through elimination/return). |
| **🠒 In range** (arrow icon) | The target space/intersection must be at the indicated **distance** (e.g., 3) or **within a range** (e.g., 0–2) from your Legend. |
| **👁 In Line of Sight** (eye icon) | Your Legend must have **unblocked LoS** to the target. |

> **Smoke Launcher example (p. 36):** Bangalore places a Smoke miniature (a **Free** action) at a **free intersection within range 0–5**, with **no level restriction** and **no LoS required.** The annotated photos show legal/illegal intersections around connected Supply Bins, Walls, Buildings, and a Dome of Protection (e.g., a miniature may not be placed "inside" a 90° connected angle, as it would cut across the connected objects).

### Zones (p. 37)
Some abilities create a **Zone** around their miniature (e.g., Smoke Launcher, Dome of Protection). A Zone is **diamond-shaped**, with edges connecting the **four vertices** of the miniature's space, **at the same level** as the miniature (it does **not** extend to other levels).

All Legends/Objects on **spaces crossed by the Zone's edge** are **in the Zone** (intersections are unaffected). A space can be in **more than one Zone.** A Zone alters rules in its area per the Ability card; certain Zone effects apply **only if:**
- at least **one Legend is in the Zone**, **or**
- the **LoS crosses one of the Zone's edges** and the **LoS Upper space is at the same level as the Zone** (the action is performed "through the zone"; for LoS Upper space, see p. 24).

> **Examples (p. 37):** A LoS that **crosses** a Smoke Zone's edge incurs the Smoke effect (e.g., **+10 To-Hit Difficulty**); a LoS that only **runs along** an edge does not. A Legend in **two** overlapping Zones is affected by **both.** A Smoke Zone on a **different level** than the LoS Upper space does **not** apply. ⚠ **[Source note:** the third Zone example on p. 37 is garbled/repetitive in the original; the intended rule is that **Zones don't extend to upper/lower levels.**]**

### Time properties (p. 38) — **green** labels (no time label ⇒ resolved immediately)
- **`DURATION X`** — the effect becomes active **immediately** and lasts **X turns.** Place the ability token **below the Initiative track, X turns ahead** (and **below** any tokens already there). When the Initiative marker reaches it (in **Resolve Initiative Marker Effects**), the effect **ceases.**
- **`DELAY X`** — the effect resolves **X turns after** the ability is performed. Place the token **below the Initiative track, X turns ahead** (below existing tokens). When the marker reaches it (in Resolve Initiative Marker Effects), the effect **triggers.**

> **Duration example (p. 38):** Bangalore's **Smoke Launcher** (Duration 3): place the mini (creates a Zone); place the token below the Initiative track **3 turns ahead**; 3 turns later, on Resolve Initiative Marker Effects, the Smoke **ceases**, the mini returns to Bangalore, and the token goes to the Cooldown track.
> **Delay example (p. 38):** Gibraltar's **Defensive Bombardment** (Delay 2): place the mini in range (0–5); token **2 turns ahead**; 2 turns later it **triggers** (damages all Legends in its AoE), then the mini returns and the token goes to Cooldown.

### Cooldown (pp. 38–39)
After use, an Ability token undergoes a **cooldown** (the card says when it starts and its length), tracked on the **Cooldown track.** Procedure:
1. Return ability-related markers/minis from the Map to your supply — **unless `PERSISTENT`** (those stay on the Map). (p. 38)
2. Place the Ability token on the **Cooldown track position shown on the Ability card.** (p. 38)
3. Each **Transition phase**, move all Cooldown-track tokens **1 space left.** (p. 39)
4. When a token moves **off** the track, cooldown is **complete**: place it on its Ability card — usable again. (p. 39)

> **Example (p. 39):** A Smoke Launcher with a **2-round** cooldown is placed on position **2**; each Transition phase it shifts left, and when it leaves the track it returns to the card.

### Card labels glossary (p. 40)
- **`FREE ACTION` / `SPECIAL ACTION: 1 AP` / `SPECIAL ACTION: 2 AP`** — AP to spend during your activation: Free = 0; Special = 1 or 2.
- **`ENHANCE—SHOOT`** — played during a Shoot to enhance it (e.g., +damage per Hit); **only 1** per Shoot (Instants with "Play as Enhance—Shoot" count toward the limit).
- **`RUSH` / `MOVE 1 / +1 MP`** — once per activation, no AP: discard a Feat to Move 1 or gain +1 MP on a Move (the Feat's main effect is ignored).
- **`REACTION` / `BEFORE FIRING` / `AFTER FIRING` / `SQUAD`** — react to an enemy Shoot (optional); keywords as above; one Before-Firing and/or one After-Firing Reaction per Shoot.
- **`INSTANT`** — playable during **any** Legend's activation if the card's condition is met.
- **`DURATION X` / `DELAY X`** — when an ability's effect starts/ends relative to its turn (tracked by the token placed X turns ahead below the Initiative track); some cards have multiple labels resolved in sequence.
- **`COOLDOWN: X`** — the Cooldown-track space where the token goes once the ability is fully resolved.
- **`PASSIVE`** — Weapon cards only: a permanent effect usable when shooting with that weapon; Passive effects resolve **first** among multiple cards.

## 19. The Database — Terminology (pp. 39–40)

- **Any Legend from your squad** — a single Legend in your squad, **including yourself.**
- **Each Legend from your squad** — **all** Legends in your squad, **including yourself.**
- **Place a miniature on a free space/edge/intersection** — a miniature with `OCCUPY SPACE` / `OCCUPY EDGE` / `OCCUPY INTERSECTION` occupies that grid feature (it's no longer free).
- **In the zone** — miniatures with the `ZONE` attribute generate a **diamond-shaped Zone** (edges connect the four vertices of the miniature's space); Legends/Objects on spaces crossed by the Zone edge are **in the Zone** (intersections unaffected); a space can be in multiple Zones.
- **Shooter** — the Legend performing a Shoot action.
- **Target** — the Legend a Shoot/Melee Attack is performed against, or the Object designated for a Destroy.
- **Fire Rate** — number of cards revealed on the Aim board. **Single number** (e.g., "2") = number of **slots** (one card each). **Pair** (e.g., "1+3") = first = **slots**, second = **cards per slot.**
- **Shot / First shot** — a **Shot** = all Aim cards in a **single slot** when resolving a Shoot; the **first shot** = all cards in the **first slot.**
- **Recoil X** — place the first shot's cards as usual; then, for each Recoil effect in play, shift the next Aim card(s) **1 additional slot right** (Recoil 2 = shift 2 slots right).
- **Each Aim card with [icon] counts as not having that icon** — treat those cards as if without the icon (its triggered effects don't apply).
- **Each Aim card with [icon] is a Hit/Miss** — treat those cards as automatic Hit/Miss; if an effect in play disregards the icon, the automatic Hit/Miss does not apply.
- **After the Deal Damage step** — the effect activates right after Deal Damage; simultaneous effects resolve in order of play (**Passive effects first**, then others).
- **Eliminate an enemy** — an enemy is eliminated when their **Health reaches 0.**
- **When a Legend enters a space** — triggers when a Legend enters a space (via Move, relocation, Push, Respawn, etc.); during a Move it triggers **the moment the Legend enters**, even if the space is occupied and they can't end there.
- **Cards** — Ability, Feat, and Weapon cards use labels, keywords, and short sentences (overview = the [card-labels glossary, p. 40](#card-labels-glossary-p-40)).

## 22. Quick Recap (p. 46)

**Legend Loadout** — equip per the chosen Game Mode. **Map Drop** — enter the Map per the chosen Game Mode.

**Final Encounter — each ROUND:** Red squad's Turn → Blue squad's Turn → Transition phase.

**Each TURN:**
- **Initiative Marker Effects:** resolve any effects below the Initiative marker.
- **Legend Activation:** each Legend (any order) activates.
- **Advance the Initiative marker** 1 space right; if it lands on the Transition phase, resolve it.

**Transition phase:** Ring `HEALTH` damage (bypasses Shields) to Legends outside the Ring limits (amount per Round position, shown below the Round track) → each Legend draws 1 Feat → shift all Cooldown tokens 1 left (return finished ones) → advance the Round marker 1 right and resolve its position → flip all Legend Draft cards face up → advance the Initiative marker to the next squad's turn.

**Contiguity:** Distance 1 (any space one step away, ignoring level/obstacles) · Adjacent (distance 1, same level, no obstacle between) · Neighboring (distance 1, same or lower level, no obstacle between).

**Line of Sight:** Standing on level 2: LoS is blocked **only to the spaces immediately behind a level-1 `BLOCKAGE`** crossed by the LoS; short-distance and level-2→level-0 rules also block nearby spaces (as illustrated).

## Appendix: Noted source inconsistencies & ambiguities

These were found while transcribing; the rulebook itself is the authority, but flag them when answering rules questions.

1. **Longbow DMR — Headshot Damage (p. 26 vs. p. 30).** The Longbow **card** shows Headshot Damage **18**; the worked damage example on p. 30 uses **14** ("10 + 10 + 14 = 34"). The example is internally self-consistent around 14/34, but conflicts with the card. **Treat the card value (18) as authoritative.**
2. **Optic "3× HCOG Ranger" (p. 43).** Token **art shows −10**, prose says **−15** (range 4–6). Transcribed as **−15**; verify against the physical token.
3. **Stage 2: Map Drop numbering (pp. 13–14).** The printed numbered list reuses "1a/1b" across what are really **two phases** (place at the Entrance board, then enter the Map). The two-phase structure here matches the p. 14 example.
4. **Zone example #3 (p. 37).** The third worked Zone example is garbled/repetitive in the source. The governing rule is that **Zones do not extend to upper or lower levels.**
5. **Aim-board example distance (p. 28).** The Longbow Aim-board example states the target is at **distance 6** (within the Longbow's "6+" Optimal Range, so no range penalty); the value is incidental to the rule being shown.
6. **Component thumbnail values (p. 4).** Small loot-token values printed on the **components** page are representative art (e.g., a Body Shield thumbnail reading "EQUIP +8"); the authoritative values are in the **Loot Tokens** database (pp. 41–43), e.g., Body Shield equip-restore **10/10/20**.
7. **Database page in the printed Contents (p. 2).** The Contents lists "The Database … 39" (Terminology 39, Loot tokens 41, Icons 44) — confirmed against the section pages.
